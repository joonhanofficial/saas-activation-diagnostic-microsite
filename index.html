Until You Segment It

The headline activation rate was not the full story.

A stable aggregate activation rate can hide where product adoption is stronger or weaker. The business question was not whether activation existed, but where activation quality changed by acquisition source and plan tier.

From unstable timing logic to a stronger activation rule.

Timing logic tested

The first instinct was to measure activation through time-to-first-usage logic.

Timing logic rejected

This method did not hold up cleanly once the tables were joined. A large share of subscriptions showed first usage earlier than subscription start, which made a simple time-based activation definition unreliable.

Usage depth summarized

The analysis shifted to focus on usage records and distinct feature use to reflect deeper product engagement.

Final activation rule selected

A stricter activation rule based on usage depth made the metric more defensible, reflecting true engagement rather than timing artifacts.

Segmentation prepared for Tableau

Built the final subscription-level analysis table and exported to CSV to build segmented views by referral source, plan tier, and interaction.

The average was not wrong. It was incomplete.

The aggregate rate gave a starting point, but the useful signal appeared only after segmentation. Activation quality was not just a product metric. It was shaped by acquisition source, plan tier, and the interaction between them.
